padam singh of ratlam
ratlam was one of the first commercial cities established in central india. the city quickly became known for trading in opium, tobacco, and salt, as well as for its bargains called "sattas". before the opening of the rajputana state railway to khandwa in 1872, there was no better place to trade than in ratlam.
the city is known for its love of food, its taste particularly the world popular salty snack 'ratlami sev'. the purity of gold jewellery and saree market of ratlam outshines in india.
