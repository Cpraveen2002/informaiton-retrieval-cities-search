during the mahabharata period in ancient india, a king named rewat had a daughter named rewati. the father used to call her rewa, and founded a village "rewa wadi" named after her. wadi and wada mean a neighbourhood (small and big, respectively) in hindi and many other indian languages. when rewa married balram, elder brother of krishna, the king donated the village "rewa-wadi" to his daughter. in the course of time, the name rewa-wadi became rewari. the royal family of rewari traces it's descend from lord krishna.
