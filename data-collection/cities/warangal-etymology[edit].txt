etymology[edit]
during the 160 years of kakatiya rule between 1163 - 1323, warangal was referred with various names like orugallu, ekasila nagaram, or omatikonda all these means a 'single stone' referring to a huge granite boulder present in the warangal fort. when the kakatiya dynasty was defeated by delhi sultanate in 1323, ruler juna khan conquered the city and renamed it as sultanpur. later musunuri nayaks recaptured warangal in 1336 a. d. and named it orugallu again.
