history[edit]
a folio from the baburnama: an awards ceremony in sultan ibrāhīm lodi's court before an expedition to sambhal
sambhal has been an urban center for hundreds of years. it was a prominent town during the medieval period. the painting shown here is a folio from the baburnama, and depicts an award ceremony in sultan ibrahim lodi's court before an expedition to sambhal in the early 16th century. sambhal is said to have flourished under the rule of akbar but subsequently deteriorated in popularity when akbar's grandson shah jahan was made in charge of the city, and the capital of the province was shifted to moradabad.
